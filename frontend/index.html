Considering images, paintings, or photographs prompts us to imagine the untold stories and emotions they may contain. Unfortunately, alternative texts, particularly essential for the visually impaired, often provide minimal details, missing the opportunity to fully engage the senses. It's common for these brief descriptions to lack the depth needed to convey the richness of visual experiences. There's a need for improvement in crafting alternative texts that not only cover the basics but also stimulate a more comprehensive understanding. Striving for more enriching alternative texts could create a bridge between visual and non-visual experiences, ensuring that art appreciation is accessible to everyone, regardless of visual ability.
